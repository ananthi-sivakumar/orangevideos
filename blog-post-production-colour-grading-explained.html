What This Means for Your Brand

Every video production decision carries strategic weight. The format you choose, the platform you optimise for, the story you tell — each of these decisions either amplifies or diminishes your brand's ability to connect with the right audience at the right moment.

The brands that win with video aren't necessarily the ones with the biggest budgets. They're the ones with the clearest thinking about what they want their audience to feel, understand, or do after watching. That clarity — more than production value — is what separates the videos that drive results from the ones that disappear into the scroll.

Key Insights from Orange Videos

Start with Audience, Not Product

The most common mistake we see in video briefs is leading with what the brand wants to say rather than what the audience needs to hear. The best creative work begins with a genuine, specific insight about the audience — what they believe, what they fear, what they aspire to — and builds outward from there.

Choose One Platform and Own It

Multi-platform distribution is a goal for optimisation, not production. When you're making creative decisions, anchor to one primary platform and one primary moment of consumption. A video designed for lean-back YouTube viewing will feel wrong on Instagram. Make it right for one context first, then adapt intelligently.

Measure What Matters

Video metrics are easy to collect and easy to misread. Match the metric to the objective, not the other way around. If your goal is brand awareness, measure recall. If it's consideration, measure intent lift. If it's conversion, measure downstream attribution.
